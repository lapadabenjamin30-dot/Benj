Library History

Info Section

The oldest known library, dating back to around 2500 BCE in ancient Mesopotamia, held thousands of clay tablets with cuneiform writing—showing that libraries have been preserving human knowledge for thousands of years.

Details Section

Libraries are more than just places to borrow books—they’re treasure troves of history and knowledge. The Library of Congress in Washington, D.C. has over 170 million items, including books, maps, and recordings.
